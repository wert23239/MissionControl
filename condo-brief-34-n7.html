Bottom line

This is financially possible, but the key tradeoff is monthly cash flow.

Current rent is about $3,000/month. Buying this place likely means about $5,900–$6,100/month cash out at a 30% down payment, before any tax refund/withholding adjustment.

Plain English: it may feel like a ~$4.3k–$4.8k/month after-tax cost, but the bank account still has to handle roughly ~$6k/month.

After-tax view

If cash payment is about $6,000/month, annual cash out is about $72k.

If mortgage-interest deduction saves around $20k/year, that is about $1,667/month.

$6,000 − $1,667 ≈ $4,333/month after-tax cash cost.

This is why it is not crazy economically, but it still requires the cash flow to comfortably pay ~$6k/month.

Why the 421-a abatement matters

The abatement is a temporary property-tax discount. The listing shows taxes around $3/month and 421-a expiring in 2036.

  • Good: keeps monthly payment much lower for roughly 10 years.
  • Risk: taxes can rise when the abatement phases out/expires.
  • Ask before buying: get the exact abatement schedule and projected unabated taxes from the broker/attorney.
